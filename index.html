Meet B01lers: The Purdue's Elite Cybersecurity Team

B01lers Figure 1. winning first place at Raymond James CTF 2023

B01lers, formally the Purdue Capture the Flag team, is a long-standing club here at Purdue. The club competes in cybersecurity competitions. Together, the club hacks their way throughout the world, landing top spots in major competitions globally. This year, the club continues its legacy, standing top 100 in the world and top 6 in the US.

CTF is a cybersecurity competition that lets the participants sharpen their skills in security. From exploitation to digital forensics, the competition encompasses different fields in security, presenting the knowledge in bite-sized chunks. The participants can practice their craft through solving those challenges.

Figure 3. Meeting in progress - Presentation

The boot camp is a long running tradition to the b01lers club. During this month long adventure, officers guides the participants through the challenging field of cybersecurity. Each meeting consists of high level overview of the topic, followed with small coding challenges designed to test the participants' learning progress. Coming in with no prior experience, the boot camp trains the member with applicable knowledge to start the journey on this adventure. Every year, b01lers attracts hundreds of new members through this boot camp program.

During normal club meetings, people came together to share their knowledge and compete in CTFs together. Routinely, there are topic sessions for members to teach each others. Afterall, teaching others helps you learn better as well.

One particular themes across the meetings are the unique culture and atmosphere among the members. Being hackers in spirit, the club members resonates together in nerdiness and quirks. The extensive meme culture incorporated into the slide shows, giving every participants an unforgettable memory. In the photo above you can see a great example of how this informality shines.

Figure 5. Meeting in progress - Discussion

Aside from the topic sessions, the club's main activity is to compete in CTF, as the name suggests. The members spreads throughout the room, finding people to collaborate on the challenges. As the type of challenges change, people bundles up into different groups that have different knowledge. This further enhances the communication among the members.

More often than not, CTF will propose challenges that are novel or distinct from school course work. This requires us to learn new techniques on the fly, applying our general knowledge in cybersecurity in a new form to solve those challenges. Therefore, the participants can practice learning new concepts, and how to apply those concepts in a different setting. This improves people's problem solving skills, which is highly valuable both in the job market and in real life.
